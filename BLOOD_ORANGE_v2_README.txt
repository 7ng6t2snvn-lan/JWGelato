📋 RECIPE #122 - BLOOD ORANGE SORBET v2 TEST (4kg)

INGREDIENTS:
  1. Blood Orange Purée v2 (sweetened, 40% sugar): 1000g
  2. Sucrose: 424g
  3. Dextrose: 200g
  4. Glucose Powder DE42: 176g
  5. Inulin: 95g
  6. Tara Gum: 13g ← ONLY stabilizer
  7. Citric Acid: 20g
  8. Water: 2072g

TOTAL: 4000g

STATS:
  ✓ Sugar: 29.4% (target 28-30%)
  ✓ Solids: 31.9% (target 31-35%)
  ✓ PAC: ~55
  ✓ POD: ~35

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

🔄 KEY DIFFERENCES: v1 vs v2

| Component | v1 (Boiron) | v2 (Sweetened) |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Purée | 1600g (10.6% sugar) | 1000g (40% sugar) |
| Sucrose | 565g | 424g |
| Dextrose | 265g | 200g |
| Glucose DE42 | 240g | 176g |
| Stabilizers | 8g tara + 5g guar + 2g kappa | 13g tara ONLY |
| Water | 1220g | 2072g |
| TOTAL | 4000g | 4000g |

WHY LESS PURÉE IN v2:
  • New purée has 40% sugar vs Boiron's 10.6%
  • Using same amount would oversweeten
  • 1000g provides sufficient flavor + color
  • Extra water compensates for lower purée volume

WHY TARA GUM ONLY:
  • Chef preference: tara gum only
  • New purée already contains guar + xanthan
  • 13g tara ≈ equivalent to old 15g total stabilizers
  • Prevents over-stabilization and "snotty" texture

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

📝 METHOD (v2):

STEP 1 - PREP STABILIZER:
  - Mix 13g tara gum with 50g of the sucrose
  - Whisk thoroughly to prevent clumping

STEP 2 - HEAT WATER WITH ACID:
  - Heat 2072g water to 40-45°C
  - Dissolve 20g citric acid completely

STEP 3 - ADD STABILIZER:
  - Whisk tara/sugar blend into acidified water
  - Ensure fully dissolved, no lumps

STEP 4 - ADD SUGARS:
  - Add remaining 374g sucrose
  - Add 200g dextrose
  - Add 176g glucose DE42
  - Whisk until dissolved

STEP 5 - HIGH-SHEAR INULIN:
  - Add 95g inulin while blending
  - Use immersion blender for 2-3 minutes
  - Must be fully hydrated

STEP 6 - ADD COLD PURÉE:
  - Add 1000g COLD sweetened blood orange purée
  - DO NOT HEAT - keep raw!
  - Blend smooth, strain if needed

STEP 7 - AGE & CHURN:
  - Age 4-12 hours at 4°C (overnight ideal)
  - Churn according to machine
  - Blast freeze to -18°C
  - Serve at -12°C to -14°C

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

💡 FLAVOR PROFILE (v2):

EXPECTED TASTE:
  • Rich, creamy from 95g inulin
  • Bright blood orange with berry notes
  • Balanced sweetness (not cloying despite sweetened purée)
  • Clean acidic finish from 20g citric acid
  • Slightly less intense orange than v1 (less purée)

TEXTURE:
  • Smooth, no gumminess (tara only)
  • Gelato-like creaminess from inulin
  • Scoops well at -12°C to -14°C

⚠️ CRITICAL NOTES:

NEW PURÉE SPECS (from label):
  • Contains added cane sugar (~12g per 59mL)
  • Total sugar: ~40% (24g per 59g serving)
  • Already contains guar gum + xanthan gum
  • Diluted with water (not pure fruit like Boiron)
  • Citric acid already present

TARA GUM ONLY:
  • Do NOT add guar or kappa
  • Purée already has gums built in
  • 13g tara provides adequate stabilization
  • Prevents texture issues from over-gumming

CITRIC ACID:
  • 20g based on Chef testing with v1
  • May need adjustment depending on purée batch
  • New purée already has citric acid added
  • Taste after aging, adjust if needed

🔧 TROUBLESHOOTING:

TOO SWEET:
  • Reduce sucrose by 50g, increase water by 50g
  • Or add 5-10g more citric acid

NOT ENOUGH ORANGE FLAVOR:
  • Increase purée to 1200g
  • Reduce water by 200g
  • Rebalance sugars if needed

GUMMY TEXTURE:
  • Reduce tara gum to 10g
  • Purée's built-in gums may be sufficient

TOO SOFT/WON'T FREEZE:
  • Reduce water by 100-200g
  • Increase glucose DE42 by 50g
