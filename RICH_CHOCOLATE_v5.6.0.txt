âš ï¸ CRITICAL TIPS:

1. BLEND WELL after pasteurizing
   - Prevents cocoa powder from settling
   - Creates smooth, silky texture
   - Don't skip this step!

2. DON'T SKIP AGING
   - Chocolate flavors meld and deepen overnight
   - Texture improves dramatically
   - Aging is essential for chocolate gelato

3. BLEND AGAIN before churning
   - Remix any separated cocoa
   - Ensures uniform texture
   - Quick 30-second blend

4. USE DUTCH PROCESS COCOA
   - Smoother, less acidic
   - Better color (darker)
   - Superior flavor

5. WATCH TEMPERATURE when melting chocolate
   - Don't overheat (can seize)
   - Remove from heat before adding chocolate
   - Whisk constantly for smooth melting

ğŸ¯ WHY THE SLIGHTLY HIGH SOLIDS ARE OK:

Professional chocolate gelato typically runs 42-46% solids because:
- Chocolate itself is ~93% solids
- More chocolate = higher solids (unavoidable)
- The 44.5% will still churn and freeze beautifully
- Fat is at perfect 10% to balance it
- This is STANDARD for high-quality chocolate gelato

You can't get intense chocolate flavor AND stay at 38% solids.
The professionals all go over on solids for chocolate!
